3. GEPA: Reflective prompt evolution can outperform reinforcement learning
(81 points, 20 comments)
[AI relevance: 2, keywords: reinforcement learning]

This article introduces GEPA (Genetic-Pareto), a novel algorithm for optimizing prompts in complex, multi-module AI systems. Unlike traditional reinforcement learning (RL) approaches, GEPA employs a language-driven, evolutionary strategy. Its core innovation is "reflective prompt mutation," where a language model analyzes its own performance, including reasoning steps, tool usage, and evaluation feedback, to diagnose failures and propose targeted improvements to its instructional prompts. GEPA uses a genetic algorithm with Pareto selection to maintain a diverse set of high-performing prompts, preventing the optimizer from getting stuck in local optima.

The results show that GEPA outperforms the RL baseline GRPO by an average of 10%, while using up to 35 times fewer system executions ("rollouts"). GEPA also surpasses the state-of-the-art prompt optimizer MIPROv2, and surprisingly demonstrates that evolving detailed instructions alone can be more effective than optimizing few-shot examples. This approach makes adapting powerful AI systems more practical and affordable, especially in settings where data is scarce or system executions are expensive.

Source: https://arxiviq.substack.com/p/gepa-reflective-prompt-evolution
